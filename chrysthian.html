Who am I?

I'm a guy who loves games, all of them, to a point where playing them was not enough, I had to create them. And I do that for fun, as a (very nerdy) hobby. Some people write, some people play music. I try to make games.

I live in Brazil, where game development is not widely known and not seen as a real job. Very few places offer jobs on the area and almost none of them offer decent or reliable oportunities. Currently I work as a programmer (always a programmer at heart) developing web applications that are not nearly related to games, but I have bills to pay, you know...

After undergraduating on a course of Systems Analysis, I took 2 years of formal education on game development with awesome teachers and professionals. Some of them created and published games here in Brazil and worldwide, reaching small to moderate success.

During my studies I think I've learned a lot of things, some of them are perfectly appliable both inside and outside of a game development scenario. Like Einstein said: "As our circle of knowlege expands, so it does the circumference of darkness surrounding it."

This is a place where I try to share what I've learned and try not to forget them myself. I do not know everything or assume someone does, and I might post things that after a while are proven imprecise, incorrect or un-optimized, but I'll try to fix and clarify those as soon as possible, with help of you out there.

Bear with me that most of what I write is based on my learning experiences and I'm just passionately curious about lots of things.
